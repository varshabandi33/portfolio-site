Mechanically Enhanced Prosthetic Foot

The focus of this project was to design and validate a 3D-printed prosthetic foot made from PLA using Finite Element Analysis (FEA) simulation and introduced design improvements to enhance its structural stiffness under realistic loading conditions.

  • Material Properties of PLA:
    • Young’s Modulus: 463.37 MPa
    • Yield Stress: 16.8 MPa
    • Ultimate Stress: 28.89 MPa
    • Density: 1.25 g/cm³
  • Initial Validation of SolidWorks Simulation:
    • A 190 N load applied to ankle in both hand calculations and SolidWorks.
    • Hand-calculated displacement: 0.281 m
    • SolidWorks displacement: 0.0117 m
    • Differences are due to simplified hand model assumptions (smaller moment of inertia).
  • Trend Verification:
    • Both the hand calculations and SolidWorks results showed that displacement decreased as applied force decreased, confirming the model behaved as expected under applied load.

Results:
