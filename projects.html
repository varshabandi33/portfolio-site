Mechanically Enhanced Prosthetic Foot

This project explores the design and mechanical validation of a simplified prosthetic foot model. Prosthetics play a vital role in restoring or enhancing human mobility, and understanding the mechanical properties of their materials is essential to ensure functionality and durability. Using PLA filament and results from tensile testing, a custom material profile was created for use in SolidWorks simulations. Finite Element Analysis (FEA) was then performed to analyze how the prosthetic responds to realistic loading—specifically a 190 N force applied at the ankle. To verify the accuracy of the simulation, results were compared against manual hand calculations. This comparison helped confirm the reliability of FEA for predicting structural performance.

Key Findings

Material Properties of PLA:

  • Young’s Modulus: 463.37 MPa
  • Yield Stress: 16.8 MPa
  • Ultimate Stress: 28.89 MPa
  • Density: 1.25 g/cm³

Initial Validation of SolidWorks Simulation:

  • A 190 N load applied to ankle in both hand calculations and SolidWorks.
  • Hand-calculated displacement: 0.281 m
  • SolidWorks displacement: 0.0117 m
  • Differences are due to simplified hand model assumptions (smaller moment of inertia).

Trend Verification:

  • Both hand calculations and SolidWorks showed that displacement decreased as applied force decreased, confirming realistic model behavior.

Design Improvements:

  • Redesigned with an I-beam cross-section to increase moment of inertia.
  • Widened and filleted ankle base for improved load distribution.
  • Ultimate Stress: 28.89 MPa
  • Overall volume constrained to 11,800.37 mm³

Results:
